# Day 029 - Build

**Date:** 2026-07-20
**Pillar:** Build

## Tidbit

Writing rows against my own specs found problems reading the specs never would have. Two of them. The first
I already knew was coming: review latency needs a handoff time, and the log spec's required fields did not
carry one. Defining a metric around a missing field is easy, and reading one around it is impossible, and
you feel the difference the moment you try to subtract two dates from a row that stores one. The second was
not on any list. The queue SOP calls a returning build a fresh handoff and sends it to the back of the line,
while the log spec keeps one row per handoff and freezes the first outcome, and those two readings quietly
disagree the instant someone writes the row. Both documents are correct on their own, and together they do
not say what a returning build does to a record. That gap survived four weeks of careful cross-checking and
took one attempt at real rows to surface. The honest version of the lesson is smaller than it sounds: I
authored these rows against my own definitions, so most of the awkward cases were ones I had already
anticipated. One was not, and real rows would produce that kind at a rate one author cannot fake.

## Shipped

`examples/a-populated-log-and-queue.md` (v1): the first records in this repo with rows instead of formulas.
A review log of twelve handoffs, a queue snapshot of four builds still waiting read on Jul 20, and the three
review-side metrics computed off those rows with the work shown. The readings: send-back rate 3 / 12 = 25
percent (75 percent first-pass approval, 6 clean and 3 carrying a fix list); review latency a median of 3.5
days over 12 first reviews, with an oldest-waiting age of 19 days and 4 builds still waiting; review miss
rate 1 / 5 audited = 20 percent, and the one overturn is a false approval. The payoff is what the numbers
say together: read one at a time, two of the three look like an ordinary queue, and the set says otherwise.
The latency median is silent on a build that has waited 19 days, because it describes the twelve that
finished, which is the survivorship trap showing up in rows rather than in a caution paragraph. The miss
rate catches an approval sitting inside that clean 75 percent that a cold second look reversed. Two of the
rows are builds this repo already traced: Day 022's rubber-stamped build is the false approval, and Day
015's is a later handoff. `sops/review-log-spec.md` goes to **v2** alongside it, adding the fifth required
field, Handed off, closing the prerequisite review latency named, refreshing two claims that went stale when
the miss rate shipped, and naming the returning-build gap.
