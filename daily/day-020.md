# Day 020 - Integrate

**Date:** 2026-07-11
**Pillar:** Integrate

## Tidbit

Week 3 was one long move: build a control loop around the review, the stage last week's loop rested on
trust. Laying the five pieces in order on the integrate day is where I saw the move's real shape and its
honest ceiling. Adding a watcher relocates trust one ring out rather than removing it: the review watches
the build, the audit watches the review, and there is always an outer edge, an auditor nobody audits,
taken on faith. That is the actual shape of every control system, not a flaw in this one. You cannot
reach zero trust, you can only push the trusted spot to where abusing it is cheapest to detect and a
careful pass is the easier path. The map is what turned five separate reactions to a gap into one ring
wrapped around last week's weak spot, which is the whole reason the integrate day keeps earning its
place.

## Shipped

`integration/week-03-the-loop-watching-itself.md` (v1): week 3's five pieces mapped as one loop that
watches another loop. Five stages, each pointing to its piece: see where the week-2 loop rests on trust
(the worked example, which named the honest-reviewer assumption), re-review the reviewer (the spot-audit),
keep the miss a calibration (the miss conversation), record every review (the review log), and measure
and feed back (the review miss rate, read next to the send-back rate). The seams section makes the ring
explicit: the example turns an abstract assumption into a target, the conversation is what a miss is
worth, the log is the one row three readers share, and the miss rate points the fix back at the review.
It ties to the prior two maps, showing this loop wraps week 2's review stage and closes the exact gap
week 2 named, whether the review itself is real. And it stays honest about the ceiling: wrapping a loop
around the reviewer pushes the trusted spot out one ring rather than removing it, because there is always
an outer edge, an auditor nobody audits. The gaps it names for later: the log is a spec not a running log
so the miss rate has no data, it is still one build and one reviewer at a time, and none of it has run
against a build anyone actually cared about.
