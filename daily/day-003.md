# Day 003 - Enable

**Date:** 2026-06-24
**Pillar:** Enable

## Tidbit

When a tool does not get used, the instinct is to blame the tool or the person. Usually it
is neither. People move through predictable stages when something new shows up: they hear
about it, they try it once, they use it when reminded, they reach for it on their own, and
eventually they show someone else. Most rollouts stall because they treat all of these as one
step. If you know which stage someone is at, you know what they need next, and adoption stops
being a vague hope and becomes something you can move one stage at a time.

## Shipped

`enablement/tool-adoption-stages.md` (v1): a plain model of the five stages a person moves
through when they pick up a new tool, with what each stage looks like, why people get stuck
there, and the one thing that moves them up.
