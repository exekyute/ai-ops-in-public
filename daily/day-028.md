# Day 028 - Reflect

**Date:** 2026-07-19
**Pillar:** Reflect

## Tidbit

Four weeks in, the reflect day has a rhythm: name the week's job, then name the wall it hit. Week 4's job was
scale, running the review loop with a pool instead of one reviewer, and the wall was people. I went in
thinking the problem was throughput and came out with a staffing fact no ordering rule reaches. The habit I
am keeping from the whole month is to end each week by naming the gap the work exposed, because the next
week's grade is just whether I closed it, and the gap you refuse to write down is the one you get to quietly
skip. This week the gap is the honest one to sit with: every piece I built can make the people constraint
visible and priced, and none can fill it. That is the ceiling of the thing, and pretending a document could
lift it would be the one dishonesty this repo cannot afford.

## Shipped

`log/week-04.md`: the fourth weekly synthesis, closing week 4 (Days 022 to 027). It reads the week as scale
after week 3's audit: a second worked example, the inspection-required checks, the new-reviewer guide, the
review queue, review latency, and the map that ties them together. Three lessons it keeps: the binding
constraint turned out to be people, not process, and every piece could make that constraint visible and
priced while none could fill it; independence is not free, and it fails the same way at two scales, two
checks that both eyeball the same item and a pool too small to hold three distinct people; and every metric
lies in the direction of its blind spot, where its data stops, which is why review latency has to be read
beside the oldest-waiting age and why it and the miss rate both go blind at a pool of one. The honest gaps
it names: it is all still specs and synthetic, the loop has never run with a real pool, and the part that
matters most, people who care to look, is the part no document can write. Carrying into week 5: run the loop
once with a real populated pool so the metrics get live rows, and onboard a first reviewer against the
guide.
