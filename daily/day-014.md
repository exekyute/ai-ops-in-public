# Day 014 - Reflect

**Date:** 2026-07-05
**Pillar:** Reflect

## Tidbit

The reflect day is meant to name what the week taught, not to count what it shipped. What made this
week easy to grade honestly is that week 1 ended by writing down three gaps: no owner, no rollback, no
cadence for reading the metric. That turned week 2's scorecard into one question, did those three
close, instead of a pile of artifacts to admire. They closed. I am taking that forward as a habit: end
each week by naming the gaps out loud, because the next week's honest grade is just whether you closed
the ones you named, and a gap you refuse to write down is one you get to quietly ignore.

## Shipped

`log/week-02.md`: the second weekly synthesis, closing week 2 (Days 008 to 013). It reads the week as
depth after week 1's breadth: four documents taken from v1 to v2, and two new pieces (the builder
self-check and the handoff SOP) that closed the seams, all mapped as one review-and-handoff loop. The
lessons it keeps: a v2 is worth more than a v1 because it carries a week of use inside it; a system is
only as consistent as its seams, so most of the week went to keeping documents in sync rather than
writing new ones; and a metric inherits the honesty of the process behind it, so send-back rate is only
real if the review is. The honest gaps it names for week 3: nothing checks whether the review itself is
honest, there is no worked end-to-end run of the loop, ownership and rollback have rules but no
register, and the loop still handles one build at a time rather than a queue.
