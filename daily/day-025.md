# Day 025 - Document

**Date:** 2026-07-16
**Pillar:** Document

## Tidbit

Documenting the review queue forced a truth I had been dodging for three weeks. The bottleneck I kept
calling "one reviewer is slow" is a staffing fact, and the ordering rules cannot touch it. A queue's
ordering is a knob you can always turn, and turning it feels like control, but all it decides is which
build waits longest. Whether the loop is a real review at all depends on the pool of independent people,
and no procedure conjures a third person who is not there. The apparatus needs three distinct people, and
below that it degrades quietly: two gives you a real review and no real audit, one gives you a self-check
wearing a review's name, and the trap is that the board still fills and empties either way, so it looks
like it is working. Writing the SOP is what made me split the part I can document, the shape and the rules,
from the part I can only report, that the pool is too small. An SOP earns its honesty by naming what it
cannot fix, and this queue's ceiling is people, not process.

## Shipped

`sops/run-the-review-queue.md` (v1): the SOP that closes the reviewer-queue bottleneck, open since week 2
and named in every map since. It defines the queue as the in-flight list (a build enters on an accepted
handoff, leaves when its review is logged), distinct from the review log's done list, and is careful about
the one case where a build sits on both: a sent-back build holds a frozen first-review log row while its
re-submission waits in the queue. It gives ordering rules (oldest-first, a small priority lane,
re-submissions to the back, watch the oldest-waiting age) and assignment rules (two hard, the reviewer is
never the builder and you pull from a pool, and one soft, vary who reviews whom). Its center is the
single-point section: the loop needs three distinct people, and below that it degrades, two gives a real
review but no independent audit, one gives a self-check wearing a review's name, and no ordering rule fixes
a pool that is too small. It stays honest that it cannot set your thresholds or conjure a third person, and
that the queue is still a manual board, a shape waiting for real rows like the log.
