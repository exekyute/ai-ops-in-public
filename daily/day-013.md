# Day 013 - Integrate

**Date:** 2026-07-04
**Pillar:** Integrate

## Tidbit

The integrate day is where five separate days have to become one thing, or they stay five documents.
What I found laying them in order was that I had not built five loose pieces this week, I had opened
up a single box from last week's map. Week 1 drew "review it before it ships" as one stage and named
three things it was missing: no owner, no rollback, no cadence for reading the metric. Week 2 closed
all three and expanded that one stage into a full loop, from the bar a build must meet to the number
that says whether it arrived ready, without my planning it that way day to day. The map is how I
noticed. That is the real lesson: integration is less about gluing parts together and more about
finding the shape they already make, and the honest test of a week is whether the gaps you wrote down
last week are the ones you closed this week.

## Shipped

`integration/week-02-review-and-handoff-loop.md` (v1): week 2's five pieces mapped as one loop, the
path a finished build takes from done to shipped. Five stages, each pointing to its piece: set the bar
(the standard), prep against it (the self-check), hand it off (the handoff SOP and its packet), run the
gate and decide (the checklist and reviewer SOP, three outcomes), then measure and feed back (the
send-back rate, which points a high number back at the self-check rather than the review). The seams
section makes the spine plain: the standard is one bar, and the self-check, the checklist, and the
handoff SOP are three views of it, so when the standard added ownership and rollback all three moved
the same week. It ties back to the week 1 map, showing this whole loop is that map's stage 2 opened up,
and it closes the three gaps week 1 named as missing (owner, rollback, metric cadence). The honest gaps
left for later: nothing yet measures whether the review itself is real, there is no worked end-to-end
example, ownership and rollback have rules but no registers, and the loop covers one build at a time,
not a queue waiting on one reviewer.
