# Day 027 - Integrate

**Date:** 2026-07-18
**Pillar:** Integrate

## Tidbit

Laying week 4 out in order, the same word showed up under every piece: people. Weeks 2 and 3 built the review
loop and then wrapped an audit around it, both quietly assuming one build in flight and one reviewer looking
at it. Week 4 ran that stack with many builds and many people, and the assumption broke in the open: the
second build needs a queue, routing around the builder needs a second person, knowing the review was real
needs a third. Every piece this week can make the people constraint visible and priced, and not one can fill
it. The checks name the line a reviewer must open and cannot open it for them; the queue flags a pool that is
too small and cannot conjure a third person; the guide teaches the habit and says outright it cannot install
the care. That is the shape of the whole week, and the honest version of operational maturity: process makes
the ceiling legible, and the ceiling is people, their number, their independence, and their willingness to
look.

## Shipped

`integration/week-04-the-loop-needs-people.md` (v1): week 4's five pieces mapped as one loop that takes the
review loop from one build and one reviewer to running with a pool. Five stages, each pointing to its piece:
see what one person misses (the rubber-stamped approval the audit catches), name what a person must open (the
inspection-required checks), grow the pool (the new-reviewer guide), route many builds to the pool (the
review queue and its three-people floor), and measure whether the pool keeps up (review latency, which points
back at growing the pool and closes the cycle). The seams section makes the chain explicit: the example makes
the checks necessary, the checks are what the guide teaches, the guide grows the pool the queue assigns from,
and the queue and the latency metric read the same waiting from opposite ends. It ties to the prior three
maps, showing weeks 2 and 3 assumed one build and one reviewer while this week runs at scale and hits a
staffing-and-independence constraint no file removes. And it is honest about the deepest gap: it is all still
specs and synthetic, the loop has never run with a real pool of real people, and the part that matters most,
people who care to look, is the part no document in here can write.
