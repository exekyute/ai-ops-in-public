# Day 008 - Build

**Date:** 2026-06-29
**Pillar:** Build

## Tidbit

The instinct on day eight is to start something new. The more useful move is often to go back and
make something from last week deeper, because the second pass is where you find out what the first
one missed. Writing the automation standard a second time, after a week of leaning on its ideas in
the review checklist, the SOP, and the metric, surfaced two rules that were not there and should
have been: who owns a live build, and how you back a bad change out. The v2 is worth more than the
v1 because it carries a week of use inside it.

## Shipped

`standards/automation-standards.md` taken to **v2**. Every core section now carries a short "what
good looks like" line so each rule has a visible pass condition, and two sections were added:
section 8 (Ownership) and section 9 (Change and rollback), the two gaps the week 1 integration map
named. Observability now points at the exception rate metric from Day 005, so the standard and the
analytics side reference the same number. The end of the file lists what changed.
